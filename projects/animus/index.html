What Animus Is

AI-powered web app that identifies any physical object via camera and lets you have a conversation with it as itself. Point at your microphone — it introduces itself as a precision instrument of sonic capture. Point at your mug — it has opinions about your caffeine dependency. Built in a weekend, deployed and live.

The Idea

What if the objects around you could speak? Not as assistants, not as tools — as themselves, with personality and perspective. It's a small experiment in what happens when AI understands the physical world well enough to give it a voice. The idea connects directly to larger work on spatial AI and augmented object intelligence.

What I Built

  • React + Vite frontend with a custom cyberpunk AR aesthetic — scanlines, glitch text, neon cyan/magenta, JetBrains Mono
  • Gemini Vision API for object identification and personality summary generation
  • Gemini Chat API for stateless conversation — history maintained client-side and passed per request, keeping the serverless function simple
  • Vercel serverless /api/gemini handling both vision and chat actions
  • Camera feed via browser MediaDevices API with tap-to-scan interaction
  • ObjectCard component showing identified object + opening personality line
  • ChatPanel component for full conversation with the object as itself

Why It Matters

Animus is a proof of concept for what becomes possible when AI understands the physical world — not just answering questions about it, but reasoning about what's in view and generating contextual knowledge. It's the web prototype of ideas that now live in the ARCore depth + Gemini research project.
